Overview

Northern Tanzania (Kilimanjaro & Tanga Regions)

Discover Mkomazi National Park, a hidden gem in Tanzania’s northern wilderness, where the rugged landscapes of the Sahel biome meet the iconic Tsavo West ecosystem of Kenya. This transboundary sanctuary is famed for its rare wildlife, including the elegant gerenuk, endangered African wild dogs, and reintroduced black rhinoceros.

With over 450 bird species and 18 large mammal species, Mkomazi offers an off-the-beaten-path safari experience. Its semi-arid savannahs, dotted with acacia and commiphora trees, create a striking backdrop for photography and adventure. The park’s Umba River shelters rare colobus monkeys, while its vast plains host massive herds of Beisa oryx—one of Tanzania’s last strongholds for this majestic antelope.

We craft immersive safaris with expert guides, private 4x4 vehicles, and stays at eco-friendly lodges. Whether you’re tracking rhinos in the Mkomazi Rhino Sanctuary or marveling at Kilimanjaro’s distant peaks, we ensure an unforgettable journey into Africa’s wild heart.

Things To Do

  • Game Drives: Spot rare species like gerenuk, Beisa oryx, and wild dogs on day/night drives across the park’s diverse habitats.

  • Rhino Tracking: Join rangers in the Mkomazi Rhino Sanctuary to observe critically endangered black rhinos (permit required).

  • Birdwatching: Seek endemic species like the vulturine guineafowl and migratory birds along the Umba River.

  • Walking Safaris: Explore the bush on foot with armed guides, learning tracking skills and plant medicinal uses.

  • Photography Expeditions: Capture the park’s stark beauty—golden savannahs, ancient baobabs, and wildlife against Kilimanjaro’s silhouette.

How To Get There

  • By Air: Fly into Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Moshi Airport. Charter flights land at Zange Airstrip (30 mins to park HQ).

  • By Road:

    • From Moshi: 2.5-hour drive (120 km) via the B1 highway.
    • From Arusha: 4-hour drive (200 km) through scenic Same District.

Best Time to Visit

    Mkomazi’s semi-arid climate makes wildlife viewing excellent year-round, but timing enhances specific experiences:

  • June–October (Dry Season):

    Best for wildlife spotting as animals gather at waterholes. Cooler temperatures (20–28°C).

  • January–February (Short Dry Spell):

    Ideal for birding and photography with lush landscapes post-rains.

  • November–December & March–May (Rainy Seasons):

    Fewer tourists; migratory birds arrive. Some roads may be impassable.
