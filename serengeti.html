Overview

Arusha (Starts), Serengeti (End)

Experience the breathtaking Serengeti National Park Home of Great Migration Safari, located in northern Tanzania, covering an area of 14,750 square kilometers. This world-renowned park is home to one of nature’s most incredible spectacles—the Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles embark on their annual journey across the plains in search of greener pastures.

Beyond the migration, the Serengeti offers a diverse range of wildlife including the Big Five-lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros—along with cheetahs, giraffes, and over 500 bird species. From endless savannas to scenic riverine forests, the Serengeti is a photographer's dream and a wildlife enthusiast's paradise.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-maintained vehicles, and premium accommodations, you’ll enjoy every moment of your adventure in the Serengeti. Whether you prefer camping under the stars or relaxing in luxury lodges, our customized itineraries ensure a seamless experience that leaves you with unforgettable memories.

Things To Do

  • Game Drives: Experience thrilling morning and evening game drives to witness the diverse wildlife of Serengeti, including the Big Five and the Great Migration.

  • Hot Air Balloon Safari: Enjoy a breathtaking aerial view of Serengeti's plains and wildlife at sunrise, followed by a champagne breakfast in the wild.

  • Maasai Cultural Experience: Visit an authentic Maasai village to learn about their traditional way of life, customs, and experience their vibrant dances.

  • Photography Safaris: Capture breathtaking shots of Serengeti's wildlife and landscapes with professional guides offering tips on the best angles and lighting.

How To Get There

  • Flights to Serengeti National Park: The most convenient way to reach Serengeti National Park is by flying into Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Arusha Airport (ARK). From there, you can take a domestic flight to one of the several airstrips located within the park. Popular airstrips include:

    • Seronera Airstrip: Located in the central Serengeti, this is the most commonly used airstrip for accessing the park's wildlife-rich areas.
    • Kogatende Airstrip: Situated in the northern Serengeti, this airstrip is ideal for witnessing the Great Migration and river crossings.
    • Grumeti Airstrip: Located in the western Serengeti, this airstrip is perfect for exploring the Grumeti River and its surrounding ecosystems.
    • Lobo Airstrip: Found in the northeastern Serengeti, this airstrip provides access to less crowded areas with abundant wildlife.
    Domestic flights are operated by airlines such as Coastal Aviation, Auric Air, and Regional Air, offering daily connections to Serengeti. Once you land at your chosen airstrip, our professional guide will be there waiting to pick you up, ready to take you on an unforgettable game drive through the endless plains of Serengeti. Here’s what you can expect:
    • Seamless Transfers: Our guides will meet you at the airstrip with a comfortable 4x4 safari vehicle, ensuring a smooth transition from your flight to your adventure.
    • Immediate Wildlife Encounters: As soon as you leave the airstrip, your game drive begins. The Serengeti’s iconic wildlife, including lions, elephants, giraffes, and zebras, can often be spotted just minutes after landing.
    • Expert Guidance: Our guides are highly knowledgeable about the park’s ecosystems and wildlife behavior. They’ll take you to the best spots for sightings and share fascinating insights about the Serengeti.
    • Customized Itineraries: Whether you want to focus on the Great Migration, explore the Grumeti River, or visit remote areas like Lobo, our guides will tailor the experience to your interests.
    Flying to Serengeti not only saves time but also offers breathtaking aerial views of the park’s vast landscapes. With our professional guides ready to welcome you, your adventure begins the moment you land.

  • Guided 4x4 Jeep Safari: For a seamless and enriching experience, consider traveling to Serengeti National Park with a professional guide in a 4x4 Jeep. Our guided road trips offer the following benefits:

    • Expert Knowledge: Our guides are highly experienced and knowledgeable about the park’s wildlife, ecosystems, and hidden gems.
    • Safety and Convenience: Navigating the rugged terrain of Serengeti can be challenging. Our guides ensure a safe and comfortable journey.
    • Customized Itineraries: We tailor your trip to suit your interests, whether you want to witness the Great Migration, explore the Grumeti River, or visit the remote Lobo area.
    • Local Insights: Our guides share fascinating stories about the park’s history, culture, and wildlife, enhancing your safari experience.
    Popular routes for guided 4x4 Jeep safaris include:
    • Arusha to Seronera: An 8-10 hour journey through the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, with stops at scenic viewpoints.
    • Moshi Kilimanjaro to Seronera: A 10-12 hour drive with stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro and the Tanzanian countryside.
    Our guided safaris also include visits to key park gates, each offering unique experiences:
    • Naabi Hill Gate: The main entry point to the central Serengeti, where you’ll find the iconic acacia trees and abundant wildlife.
    • Ndabaka Gate: Located in the western Serengeti, this gate is perfect for exploring the Grumeti River and its resident hippos and crocodiles.
    • Klein’s Gate: Situated in the northeastern Serengeti, this gate provides access to the less crowded Lobo area, known for its excellent wildlife viewing.
    • Bologonya Gate: Located in the northern Serengeti, this gate is close to the Mara River, where you can witness the dramatic river crossings during the Great Migration.
    With a guided 4x4 Jeep safari, you’ll experience the best of Serengeti National Park while enjoying the comfort and expertise of our professional guides.

Best Time to Visit

    Calving Season in the Southern Serengeti is one of the most spectacular times to visit. Witness the miracle of birth as thousands of wildebeest, zebra, and gazelles give birth to their young. This is also an excellent time to witness predator action, as lions, cheetahs, and hyenas take advantage of the vulnerable newborns. Don't miss the opportunity to capture breathtaking photos of baby animals and their protective mothers, making this season a must for wildlife enthusiasts.

  • June – October:

    The Dry Season is the best time for those seeking exceptional wildlife sightings in Tanzania’s Serengeti. The lack of rainfall means animals congregate around waterholes, providing excellent opportunities to observe the Big Five — lion, leopard, rhinoceros, elephant, and buffalo. The clear skies and fewer insects create perfect conditions for a safari adventure, offering uninterrupted game viewing. This period is perfect for avid photographers and nature lovers who want to capture the majestic beauty of Africa’s wildlife.

  • July – September:

    The Great Migration is a once-in-a-lifetime spectacle that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. During this time, you can witness the awe-inspiring sight of thousands of wildebeest and zebras crossing the Mara River in a dramatic and dangerous journey to find greener pastures. Along the way, predators such as crocodiles and lions await, offering an extraordinary chance to observe the raw survival instincts of both predator and prey. This event is undoubtedly one of the most thrilling experiences in the Serengeti.

  • November – December:

    Short Rains bring lush greenery and revitalized landscapes, making the Serengeti a scenic paradise. The shorter rainfall and fewer tourists create a more intimate and peaceful experience for those who seek a quieter safari. This is also the perfect time for budget-conscious travelers as rates tend to be more affordable, while still offering fantastic opportunities for wildlife viewing. Visitors can enjoy the beauty of the Serengeti’s flora and fauna without the crowds, creating a tranquil and memorable safari experience.
