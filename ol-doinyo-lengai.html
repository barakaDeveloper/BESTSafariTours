Overview

Arusha (Starts), Ol Doinyo Lengai (End)

Climb the iconic Ol Doinyo Lengai, known as the "Mountain of God" to the Maasai people. This active volcano, standing at 2,962 meters, is located in northern Tanzania and offers one of the most unique climbing experiences in the world. Ol Doinyo Lengai is the only volcano that produces carbonatite lava, a rare type of lava that flows at much lower temperatures than other volcanoes.

The trek to the summit is challenging yet incredibly rewarding, offering stunning views of the Great Rift Valley and nearby Lake Natron. Reaching the summit at sunrise is a breathtaking experience, as the surrounding landscapes are bathed in golden light.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-planned itineraries, and premium equipment, we ensure a seamless and memorable climb. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a first-time adventurer, our customized trekking packages cater to all levels of experience, leaving you with unforgettable memories.

Things To Do

  • Summit Ol Doinyo Lengai: Conquer the summit of Ol Doinyo Lengai and enjoy breathtaking views of the Great Rift Valley and Lake Natron. The climb is challenging but rewarding, especially at sunrise.

  • Explore the Crater: Discover the unique carbonatite lava flows and volcanic craters near the summit. This rare geological feature is a must-see for any adventurer.

  • Photography: Capture stunning shots of Ol Doinyo Lengai’s landscapes, volcanic craters, and sunrise views. The dramatic scenery provides endless opportunities for photography.

  • Cultural Visits: Meet the local Maasai communities and learn about their traditions, customs, and way of life. Participate in cultural activities and enjoy traditional dances.

  • Camping Adventures: Spend a night under the stars near Ol Doinyo Lengai. Enjoy the tranquility of the remote wilderness and wake up to stunning views of the volcano.

  • Wildlife Viewing: Spot wildlife like flamingos, pelicans, and other bird species around Lake Natron during your trek.

  • Visit Lake Natron: Combine your Ol Doinyo Lengai adventure with a visit to Lake Natron, known for its flamingos and unique landscapes.

How To Get There

  • Road Transfers: Ol Doinyo Lengai is accessible by road from Arusha, a 4-5 hour drive through scenic landscapes. Our professional guides will ensure a comfortable and informative journey.

  • Flights to Lake Natron: For a quicker option, take a domestic flight from Arusha to Lake Natron Airstrip. From there, a short transfer will take you to the base of Ol Doinyo Lengai.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to climb Ol Doinyo Lengai is during the dry season (June to October), when the weather is clear, and the trails are in optimal condition. The green season (November to May) offers lush landscapes but comes with occasional rain.

  • June – October:

    The dry season is perfect for climbing, with clear skies and excellent trekking conditions.

  • November – May:

    The green season is ideal for photography and enjoying the lush scenery.
