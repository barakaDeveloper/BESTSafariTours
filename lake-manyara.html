Overview

Arusha (Start), Lake Manyara (End)

Discover the beauty of Lake Manyara National Park, a peaceful paradise nestled between the Great Rift Valley and the calm waters of Lake Manyara. Though small in size, this park offers a fantastic safari adventure with stunning forests, wide-open plains, and iconic acacia trees.

Famous for its tree-climbing lions and large elephant herds, Lake Manyara is a dream for nature lovers. Birdwatchers will be thrilled by the thousands of flamingos and over 400 bird species around the lake. With breathtaking views and amazing wildlife, this park is a must-visit for any Tanzania safari.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-maintained vehicles, and premium accommodations, we ensure your Lake Manyara experience is unforgettable. Whether you’re a wildlife enthusiast, a photographer, or simply seeking tranquility, Lake Manyara has something for everyone.

Things To Do

  • Game Drives: Embark on thrilling game drives to spot Lake Manyara’s famous tree-climbing lions, elephants, buffaloes, and more. The park’s diverse landscapes make every drive a unique adventure.

  • Bird Watching: With over 400 bird species, Lake Manyara is a birdwatcher’s paradise. Spot flamingos, pelicans, storks, and many other species around the lake.

  • Walking Safaris: Explore the park on foot with expert guides, learning about its flora, fauna, and tracking techniques. Walking safaris offer a closer connection to nature.

  • Photography Safaris: Capture stunning shots of Lake Manyara’s landscapes, wildlife, and birdlife. The park’s scenic beauty provides endless opportunities for photography.

  • Lake Manyara Canoeing: Enjoy a unique perspective of the park by canoeing on Lake Manyara. Spot hippos, waterbirds, and other wildlife from the water.

  • Cultural Visits: Visit nearby Maasai villages to learn about their traditions, customs, and way of life. Participate in cultural activities and enjoy traditional dances.

  • Camping Adventures: Spend a night under the stars in one of Lake Manyara’s campsites. Enjoy the sounds of the wilderness and wake up to stunning views.

  • Night Game Drives: Experience the thrill of a night game drive to spot nocturnal animals like hyenas, leopards, and bushbabies.

How To Get There

  • Road Transfers: Lake Manyara National Park is easily accessible by road from Arusha, a 2-3 hour drive through scenic landscapes. Our professional guides will ensure a comfortable and informative journey.

  • Flights to Lake Manyara: For a quicker option, take a domestic flight from Arusha or Kilimanjaro International Airport to Lake Manyara Airstrip. From there, a short transfer will take you to the park entrance.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to visit Lake Manyara National Park is during the dry season (July to October), when wildlife congregates around the lake, offering exceptional game viewing opportunities. The green season (November to June) is ideal for birdwatching and enjoying lush landscapes.

  • July – October:

    The dry season is perfect for spotting large herds of elephants, tree-climbing lions, and other wildlife.

  • November – June:

    The green season transforms the park into a birdwatcher’s paradise, with migratory birds and vibrant scenery.
