Overview

Arusha (Starts), Ngorongoro Conservation Area (End)

Discover the breathtaking Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Africa’s most iconic destinations. Formed over two million years ago, this massive volcanic caldera spans 260 square kilometers and is home to an incredible diversity of wildlife, including the Big Five—lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros.

The crater’s unique ecosystem supports over 25,000 large animals, making it one of the most densely populated wildlife areas in the world. From the lush grasslands to the acacia forests and the alkaline Lake Magadi, the Ngorongoro Crater offers unparalleled opportunities for wildlife viewing and photography.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-maintained vehicles, and premium accommodations, you’ll enjoy every moment of your adventure in the Ngorongoro Crater. Whether you prefer camping under the stars or relaxing in luxury lodges, our customized itineraries ensure a seamless experience that leaves you with unforgettable memories.

Things To Do

  • Game Drives: Explore the crater floor on thrilling game drives to witness the diverse wildlife, including lions, elephants, and the rare black rhinoceros.

  • Walking Safaris: Take guided walking tours along the crater rim to enjoy stunning views and learn about the local flora and fauna.

  • Maasai Cultural Experience: Visit a Maasai village to learn about their traditional way of life, customs, and vibrant dances.

  • Bird Watching: Spot over 500 bird species, including flamingos, ostriches, and eagles, in the crater’s diverse habitats.

  • Photography Safaris: Capture breathtaking shots of the crater’s landscapes and wildlife with professional guides offering tips on the best angles and lighting.

How To Get There

  • Flights to Ngorongoro Crater: The most convenient way to reach Ngorongoro Crater is by flying into Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Arusha Airport (ARK). From there, you can take a domestic flight to Ndutu Airstrip, is located well within the Ngorongoro Conservation Area but right on the door step of the vast Serengeti National Park‚ Lake Ndutu, followed by a short drive to the crater.

  • Guided 4x4 Jeep Safari: For a seamless and enriching experience, consider traveling to Ngorongoro Crater with a professional guide in a 4x4 Jeep. Popular routes include:

    • Arusha to Ngorongoro: A 3-4 hour drive through the scenic Tanzanian countryside.
    • Serengeti to Ngorongoro:A 5-6 hour scenic drive through the Great Rift Valley offers breathtaking views. Alternatively, take a short flight to one of Serengeti’s airstrips, where a 4x4 safari Jeep will be ready to pick you up and take you directly to the crater.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to visit Ngorongoro Crater is during the dry season (June to September), when wildlife congregates around water sources, making it easier to spot animals. The calving season (January to February) is also a great time to visit, as you can witness newborn animals and predator action.

  • June – October:

    The dry season offers excellent wildlife viewing opportunities, with clear skies and fewer insects.

  • January – February:

    The calving season is a great time to witness newborn animals and predator action.

  • November – December:

    The short rains bring lush greenery and fewer tourists, making it a peaceful time to visit.
