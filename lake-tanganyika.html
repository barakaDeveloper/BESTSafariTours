Overview

Kigoma (Start & End)

Embark on an unforgettable adventure to Lake Tanganyika, the world’s longest freshwater lake, stretching over 673 kilometers (418 miles) across the East African Rift Valley. Surrounded by dramatic landscapes, including rugged mountains and lush forests, Lake Tanganyika is teeming with diverse wildlife and unique ecosystems, making it a paradise for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike.

Enjoy activities such as snorkeling, fishing, and kayaking, or simply relax on the pristine shores of this stunning lake. The lake is home to unique species of fish, including the famous cichlids, and provides a breathtaking backdrop to any safari adventure. Whether you want to dive into its crystal-clear waters or explore its hidden coves, Lake Tanganyika offers an adventure like no other.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-maintained facilities, and premium accommodations, we ensure your Lake Tanganyika experience is unforgettable. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, an adventure seeker, or simply looking to unwind, Lake Tanganyika has something for everyone. Its rich biodiversity and peaceful environment make it an ideal destination for relaxation and exploration.

Things To Do

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the crystal-clear waters of Lake Tanganyika, home to unique species of fish like cichlids. The lake’s underwater world is a paradise for snorkelers and divers.

  • Fishing: Try your hand at fishing in the lake, a popular activity among locals and visitors alike. The lake is known for its abundance of fish species.

  • Kayaking and Boat Rides: Glide across the serene waters of Lake Tanganyika by kayak or boat, enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Photography: Capture stunning shots of the lake’s scenic beauty, vibrant sunsets, and unique wildlife.

  • Hiking: Explore the scenic trails around the lake, offering breathtaking views of the water and surrounding landscapes.

  • Cultural Visits: Visit nearby villages to learn about local traditions, customs, and daily life. Participate in cultural activities and enjoy traditional dances.

  • Camping: Spend a night under the stars near the lake’s shores, surrounded by the sounds of nature.

  • Bird Watching: Spot a variety of bird species that inhabit the lake’s shores, including kingfishers, herons, and African fish eagles.

How To Get There

  • Flights to Kigoma: The easiest way to reach Lake Tanganyika is by flying into Kigoma Airport, followed by a short drive to the lake.

  • Ferry from Mpulungu: For a scenic journey, take a ferry from Mpulungu (Zambia) to Kigoma, offering stunning views of the lake. Our professional guides will ensure a comfortable and informative trip.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to visit Lake Tanganyika is during the dry season (June to October), when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are at their best. The green season (November to May) offers lush landscapes and is perfect for enjoying the lake’s serene beauty.

  • June – October:

    The dry season is ideal for water activities, wildlife spotting, and hiking, with clear skies and comfortable temperatures.

  • November – May:

    The green season transforms the area into a lush paradise, perfect for nature lovers and photographers.
