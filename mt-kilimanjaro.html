Overview

Moshi, Kilimanjaro (Starts and Ends)

Embark on the adventure of a lifetime by conquering the world’s tallest free-standing mountain—Mount Kilimanjaro. Standing at a majestic 5,895 meters, Kilimanjaro offers climbers the chance to scale the "Roof of Africa" and witness breathtaking views across Tanzania's vast landscapes.

With various trekking routes to suit different skill levels, from the scenic Marangu route to the challenging Machame path, each journey up Kilimanjaro is an unforgettable experience. Along the way, you’ll traverse through diverse ecosystems—from lush rainforests and alpine meadows to volcanic craters and snow-capped peaks—culminating in the spectacular sunrise over Uhuru Peak.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-planned itineraries, and premium equipment, we ensure a seamless and memorable climb. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a first-time adventurer, our customized trekking packages cater to all levels of experience, leaving you with unforgettable memories.

Things To Do

  • Summit Uhuru Peak: Conquer the highest point in Africa and witness a breathtaking sunrise over the clouds.

  • Explore Diverse Ecosystems: Trek through lush rainforests, alpine meadows, and volcanic landscapes.

  • Photography: Capture stunning views of Kilimanjaro’s glaciers, wildlife, and panoramic landscapes.

  • Cultural Visits: Explore the vibrant culture of the Chagga people in nearby villages.

  • Camping Adventures: Experience nights under the stars in high-altitude campsites.

How To Get There

  • Flights to Kilimanjaro: The easiest way to reach Mount Kilimanjaro is by flying into Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO), located just 45 minutes from Moshi town. From there, our team will arrange a transfer to your hotel or the starting point of your trek.

  • Road Transfers: If you’re traveling from Arusha or Nairobi, you can take a scenic drive to Moshi. The journey from Arusha takes about 2 hours, while the drive from Nairobi takes approximately 6-7 hours.

Where To Stay

  • Moshi Town Lodges: Stay in comfortable lodges in Moshi before and after your trek. Popular options include:

    • Keys Hotel: A boutique hotel offering modern amenities and stunning views of Kilimanjaro.
    • Parkview Inn: A budget-friendly option with cozy rooms and a central location.

  • Mountain Campsites: During your trek, stay in well-equipped campsites along the route, such as:

    • Machame Camp: Nestled in the rainforest, offering a serene atmosphere.
    • Barranco Camp: Located at 3,900 meters, with breathtaking views of the Barranco Wall.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to climb Mount Kilimanjaro is during the dry seasons (January to March and June to October), when the weather is clear, and the trails are in optimal condition. The green season (April to May and November to December) offers fewer crowds but comes with higher chances of rain.

  • January – March:

    The first dry season offers clear skies and excellent trekking conditions.

  • June – October:

    The second dry season is ideal for climbing, with cooler temperatures and minimal rainfall.

  • April – May, November – December:

    The green season is less crowded but comes with wetter conditions, making it suitable for experienced climbers.
