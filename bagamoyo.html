Overview

Dar es Salaam (Start), Bagamoyo (End)

Step back in time with a visit to Bagamoyo Island, an island rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Located near the historical town of Bagamoyo, this island offers peaceful beaches and a chance to explore Tanzania's fascinating past.

Once a key trading post, Bagamoyo Island is filled with stories of explorers, missionaries, and traders. Visit ancient ruins, relax on pristine beaches, and enjoy the slow pace of island life.

For us, your safety and comfort are our priority. With expert guides, well-maintained facilities, and premium accommodations, we ensure your Bagamoyo Island experience is unforgettable. Whether you’re a history buff, a beach lover, or simply seeking tranquility, Bagamoyo Island has something for everyone.

Things To Do

  • Explore Ancient Ruins: Visit the island’s historic ruins, including old trading posts, missionary buildings, and colonial-era structures.

  • Relax on Pristine Beaches: Unwind on the island’s peaceful beaches, surrounded by the serene beauty of the Indian Ocean.

  • Photography: Capture stunning shots of the island’s historic sites, scenic beaches, and vibrant sunsets.

  • Boat Rides: Take a boat ride around the island to enjoy panoramic views of the coastline and spot marine life.

  • Cultural Visits: Visit nearby Bagamoyo town to learn about local traditions, customs, and daily life. Participate in cultural activities and enjoy traditional dances.

  • Swimming and Snorkeling: Dive into the warm, clear waters of the Indian Ocean and explore vibrant coral reefs.

  • Camping: Spend a night under the stars on the island, surrounded by the sounds of the ocean and nature.

  • Bird Watching: Spot a variety of bird species that inhabit the island’s shores and nearby mangroves.

How To Get There

  • Flights to Dar es Salaam: The easiest way to reach Bagamoyo Island is by flying into Dar es Salaam Airport, followed by a short drive to Bagamoyo town.

  • Road Transfers: For a scenic journey, travel to Bagamoyo by road from Dar es Salaam, a 1-2 hour drive through picturesque landscapes. Our professional guides will ensure a comfortable and informative trip.

  • Boat Transfers: From Bagamoyo town, take a short boat ride to the island, offering stunning views of the coastline.

Where To Stay

  • Beachside Camping: Experience the magic of Bagamoyo Island with guided camping safaris. Sleep under the stars, surrounded by the sounds of the ocean.

  • Lodges and Resorts: Choose from a range of accommodations, each offering unique experiences:

    • Bagamoyo Beach Resort: A luxury resort offering stunning views of the Indian Ocean and easy access to the island.
    • Kaole Beach Cottages: A boutique lodge located near the island, offering a serene and immersive experience.

Best Time to Visit

    The best time to visit Bagamoyo Island is during the dry season (June to October), when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are at their best. The green season (November to May) offers lush landscapes and is perfect for enjoying the island’s serene beauty.

  • June – October:

    The dry season is ideal for exploring historic sites, relaxing on beaches, and enjoying water activities.

  • November – May:

    The green season transforms the island into a lush paradise, perfect for nature lovers and photographers.
